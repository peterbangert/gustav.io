Bistecca alla fiorentina

or 'beefsteak Florentine style'

@peterbtechnik.

A favorite of Tuscan cuisine, the steak is grilled over a wood or charcoal fire, seasoned with salt, sometimes with black pepper, and olive oil, applied immediately after the meat is retired from the heat

@peterbtechnik.

traditionally served very rare, sometimes garnished with lemon wedges, if not accompanied by red wine, and accompanied by Tuscan beans as a side dish.

Enough introductions aside

Looks like youre in. Now you may ask, 'How do I go about getting myself one of these ?'. Heres How

Book a flight, and sit your ass down.

And enjoy some of this goddamn scenery

@peterbtechnik.

Do tourist things. Take a picture overlooking Florence. +Points for doing it with your hiking backpack

@peterbtechnik.

Go to your hotel, shower, get changed.

Now go back out and look at some of these statues.

This step is critical. You need to understand your the context of this steak.

@peterbtechnik.

You are about to spend 40 euroes for a Porterhouse. Dont make me explain what a Porterhouse is. Essentially you could throw away the rest of the cow and this cut of meat would still let you break even.

@peterbtechnik.

And if you allow yourself to empathize with Tuscany in the 1300s (Renaissance), you can assume that prime cuts were a dish for royalty. Just like these Classical Magnates. Just like you baby. Whats next.

@peterbtechnik.

Hungry enough yet? Lets find a resturaunt.

Ill have to reach out to my cousin to remember the name of our establishment. But these Rules apply nonetheless.

Atmosphere is key. The resturaunt should be part shop. If they sell dried pasta, riccota cheese, and wine.. You're headed in the right direction. These guys are pros.

+Points if they have live seafood in a tank.

Sit Down, order yourself some Tuscan Olives or beans, Maybe learn how to actually pronounce Bruschetta, or some chicken liver pattea (my recommendation). Then order yourself a goddamn steak.

Dont even try to pronounce it in italian, just point to the menu. Pair it with a Chianti. Or the house wine.
